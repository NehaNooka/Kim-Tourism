Kim, Gujarat

In the great cities we see so little of the world, we drift into our minority. In the little towns and villages there are no minorities; people are not numerous enough. You must see the world there, perforce. Every man is himself a class; every hour carries its new challenge. When you pass the inn at the end of the village you leave your favourite whimsy behind you; for you will meet no one who can share it. We listen to eloquent speaking, read books and write them, settle all the affairs of the universe. The dumb village multitudes pass on unchanging; the feel of the spade in the hand is no different for all our talk: good seasons and bad follow each other as of old. The dumb multitudes are no more concerned with us than is the old horse peering through the rusty gate of the village pound. The ancient map-makers wrote across unexplored regions, 'Here are lions.' Across the villages of turners of the earth, so different are these from us.

Kim is a census town in Surat district in the Indian state of Gujarat.

  • As of 2011 India census, Kim had a population of 18,638.

  • Males constitute 53.49% of the population.

  • Females constitute 46.51% of the population..

  • Kim has an average literacy rate of 89.25%

  • male literacy is 92.74%

  • female literacy is 85.22%.

  • In Kim, 12.38% of the population is under 6 years of age.
